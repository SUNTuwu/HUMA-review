The Yi
• The Yi was considerable diversity within the
group of 7.8 million people defined as Yi.
• They speak languages that are by no stretch of
the aural imagination mutually comprehensible.
However these languages are fairly closely
related to each other.

• Linguistics give figures of anywhere 20% to 42%
shared vocabulary between the Northern Dialect
standard and examples of the other five regional
dialects of Yi.

diversity among themselves, still seem to possess
some traits in common (lowland agriculture, Hanstyle housing, patrilocal marriage), but their society

The Miao
• Some ethnic groups referred to as Miao are
located in Guangxi, Guizhou, Yunnan, Hunan,
Sichuan, Guangdong.

• Linguistically and culturally some of these
groups are related to the Hmong (Meo) of
Thailand and Laos.
• According to 2000 census, there are 8.94
million in China.
14

15

• The term Miao 苗 referring to a culturally
and linguistically diverse category of nonHan peoples in early Chinese writings.
• In Ming period the term was applied to
many indigenous peoples of the new
southwestern frontier, and used
interchangeablly with Man 蛮 or Yi 夷, all
three terms meant “barbarians,” and did
not refer to any self-defined ethnic group.
16

• In 1741, Guizhou Gazetteer 《（乾隆）貴州通志》
names and describes 13 kinds of Miao.
• Miao subgroups are distinguished subgroups of
Yao瑶, Gelao仡佬, Mulao仫佬, Songjia宋家,
Nongjia儂家, Zhongjia仲家, and others in
Guizhou region.
• The gazetteers and the “Miao albums” 《百苗
圖》、《苗蛮圖册》 recognize that not all
barbarians are Miao, and continue to refine the
distinctions within the large Miao category.

• Competition for political office or economic
advantages is usually not between subgroups of
Miao, but between local Miao and Han or
whatever other ethnic groups in the area.
• Lack of contact and absence of competition for
local resources, these peoples are called Miao,
because they have always been called by others
as Miao.

• The popular image of the Miao shows them as a
wealthy people who spend most of their time
singing, feasting, drinking, and dancing, decked
with silver ornaments.
