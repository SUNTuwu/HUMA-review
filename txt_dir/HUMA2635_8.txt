History
• The name “Xinjiang” (New Dominion) was 
imposed by the Manchus during the Qing period, 
and reflected the imperial perspective.  
• Modern ethnic identities of the peoples of 
Xinjiang in terms of “minzu” nationalities have 
gradually taken shape only in the past 100 
years.
• Historically, rulers of China referred to the 
people in this area with name like “Rong” 戎or 
“Hu” 胡barbarians.  

14
• After 7th century, the nomadic tribes in the Eurasian 
steppes got a distinct name: Turks 突厥.
• The Turks split into a western and an eastern 
sectors.  
• The western sector included 10 tribes that occupied 
an area north of the Tianshan Mountians 天山and 
in the close neighborhood of Balkash Lake 巴爾喀
什湖.  
• These tribes divided their lands and nomadic 
communities on the basis of kinship.  

15
• Some tribes continued the old custom of 
marrying into outside tribes to form 
alliances.  
• The tribal union depended on the Khan’s 
personal authority and the relative strength 
of the dominant tribe within the union.

16
• After the western Turk khanate 西突厥汗國
collapsed, one of its branches took its place and 
organized a new nomadic kingdom.  
• They played a strong role in resisting the Arabs 
who tried to enter Central Asia from the west.
• In the 8th century, the Turks rebelled against the 
Tang dynasty.
• The Uyghurs assisted to suppress the rebellion, 
and afterward their tribal names were recorded.  

17

18
• The historical names of the Uyghurs are closely to the 
ancient Uyghurs (Hui Hu).  
• The name referred to a tribal union of nine outer groups 
before a Uyghur kingdom was established in 744 A.D. 
漠北回纥汗國（744-840）.  
• The Uyghurs’ became a strong force in north of the 
Mongolian Plateau. 
 
• In 788 A.D., the fourth khan Dunmohe of the Uyghur 
kingdom, wrote a letter to Tang emperor to request for 
the permission to change his people’s name from Hui He 
回纥to Hui Hu 回鶻, meaning eagles circling in the sky 
in the Chinese transliteration. 

19
• In the 9th century, the Uyghur kingdom 
collapsed under attacks from the nomadic 
Kirghiz.  
• A branch of the Uyghur tribes moved to 
the Pamir region 帕米爾and established a 
Karakhan kingdom.  
• The kingdom also involved some local 
ethnic groups, especially the Turk tribes.

20
• In 935 A. D., Satuk Bughra Khan converted to 
Islam and made Islam the kingdom’s official 
religion.  
• During Yuan dynasty, most documents in China 
called the Muslim Turks “Hui Hui” 回回.  
• Turfan 吐鲁番, Kashgar 喀什, and Kucha 庫車, 
all occupied by the Uyghurs, were identified as 
“places of Hui Hui.”  
• From then on, the name of the Uyghurs become 
closely tied to religion.  

21
• During the Song dynasty, some Chinese books 
even named Buddhist or Christian Uyghurs as “Hui 
Hui” 回回, causing an assumption later that the 
name had some connection with Buddhism. 
• During the 13th century, the Mongol cavalries 
entered the area south of the Tianshan Mountians.  
• Eventually, they were assimilated into the local 
culture and became Turkicized and Islamized. 

22
• Although Hui Hu or Hui He was still used 
to refer the Uyghurs in Chinese 
documents, the Uyghurs ethnic identity 
obviously changed significantly, with Islam 
as its most important symbols.
• After the Hui Hu of Gaochang 髙昌回鹘
converted to Islam, however, they were no 
longer called Hui Hu in Chinese 
documents.

28
• Sheng Shicai’s 盛世才ethnic policies in Xinjiang was to 
implement equal rights among minority ethnic groups.  
• In the early 1930s this policy was adopted to reduce 
interethnic frictions in Xinjiang.  
• Following the Soviet Union’s naming system, Sheng’s 
regime clarified and determined the names of some 
small ethnic groups in Xinjiang.  For example, Burut was 
changed to “Kirghiz.”
• Sheng Shicai confirmed the Taranchi as one of 14 
nationalities in Xinjiang.  In 1950s, however, the 
Taranchi were treated as part of the Uyghur nationality 
by the central government of the newly found PRC.

29
• During Republic of China, in 1934, Sheng 
Shicai officially abolished Chan Hui and 
Chan tou 缠頭as two official offensive 
names.  In their places Uyghurs (Weiwu’er 
in Chinese) was adopted.  
• Uyghur in their language meant “help,” 
coming from a legand that the “Uyghur” 
had been supported by some friends 
against his father.

30
• After 1949, the central government began to 
send dispatch “ethnicity recognition and 
investigation teams”.  
• The purpose was to study and research the 
factual basis for identifying China’s ethnic 
groups.  
• The ethnic identities of the Shen Shicai period 
were continued.  
• The Taranchi were merged with the Uyghur, the 
name for Russians was changed from Guihua 歸
化to Russian俄羅斯族, the name of Solon 索侖
was replaced by Daur 达斡爾族.

Pan-Turkism 泛突厥主義
• After the Turkish Revolution, Turkish nationalist leaders 
began to subdue Pan-Islamism and Pan-Turkism in their 
state.  
• In 1910, a Xinjiang activist Masud Sabri went to Turkey to 
study.  
• After he returned, Masud began to disseminate Pan-
Turkism by way of teaching mathematics and medcial 
knowledge, began use “Turk” as the name for local people.  
• The British showed a special interest in the separatist 
tendencies in southern Xinjiang and used these as 
opportunities to interfere with Xinjiang’s affairs.

• In the 1930s, an “Islam Republic of East 
Turkestan” emerged in Xinjiang and sent a 
delegation to Afghanistan.  
• The delegations mission was to ask assistance 
from the British colonial authorities in India and to 
gain international recognition.  
• After the collapse of the “Islam Republic of East 
Turkestan,” some of its supporters escaped to 
Afghanistan.

East Turkestan 
Movement

35
• The Pan-Turkists Masud and Mohammed 
Imin did not see themselves as “Uyghurs.”  
• Their view about the ethnic composition of 
Xinjiang was similar to the Chinese 
nationalist government about China.  
• The Uyghurs, Kazaks, Kirghizs, Taranchis, 
Uzbeks, Tatars, and Tajiks were not 
distinct ethnic groups in Xinjiang.  

36
• By contrast, the designation of “East Turkestan” 
represented the Russian view, which emphasized 
continuities with the other Turkic-speaking regions 
of Central Asia (West Turkestan).  
• This Russian distinction between East and West 
Turkestan incorporated both the linguistic and 
cultural continuities connecting the two regions and 
the political/colonial boundaries separating them.

Ili region 伊犁
• Ili has always been considered the richest area in 
Xinjiang and perhaps all of Central Asia.
• Traditionally it was agriculture, livestock, and 
trading center of much of northern Xinjiang.  
• Lies on the western edge of the Tianshan range 
where it meets the Kazakstan, once Russian, 
border.
• it is geographically isolated from southern Xinjiang 
and physically oriented toward Kazakstan.  

46
Ili 伊犁

47
• In 1759, the Qing encouraged 10,000 Muslim 
families to move from the Alta-shahar region to 
the Ili region.  
• Later, both Soviet Union and China viewed the 
Taranchi settlers as a distinct ethnic group, 
separate from the Uyghur.  
• In 1949, the over 80,000 Taranchis were 
reclassified as Uyghurs.  
• Ili has pose the greatest Turkic political threat to 
the Chinese government in this century.  

48
• From 1944-1949, the Ili region revolted 
against Chinese rule and established the 
Eastern Turkestan Republic with support 
from the Soviet Union. 
• Just before the establishment of the 
People’s Republic of China, the central 
leaders of the East Turkestan Republic 
died in a plane crash on their way to meet 
the Chinese Communist leaders.

49
• The tension in this region has continued to 
the present, but reached its height in 1962 
as Chinese-Soviet relations fell apart. 
 
• As a result of the failure of the Great Leap 
Forward, the Soviets helped an estimated 
60,000-120,000 Uyghurs and Kazaks to 
flee from Ili and other northern Xinjiang 
border regions to the Soviet Union.

Turpan region 吐鲁番
• The Uyghur non-Islamic Gao Chang kingdom 
settled after 840 A.D in Turpan 髙昌囬鶻.
• The Turpan oasis is the historic center of Uyghur 
culture.  
• A beautiful and fascinating place lies on the 
trade-route between Hami and Kashgar.

• Islamic life in the south, particularly in

• Women are completely veiled in brown

Summary
• The modern Uyghur identity was not “created” by the 
Chinese state, but was initiated much earlier in Russian 
Central Asia.
• Travels along the Silk Road, trade interactions, wars, 
migrations and intermarriage have shaped the local 
identities .  
• In the early 1990s, the newly independent Central Asian 
states have a strong impact on political developments for 
the Uyghurs. 
• Oases identities and oases economic-political structure set 
the basic feature for Xinjiang society.
