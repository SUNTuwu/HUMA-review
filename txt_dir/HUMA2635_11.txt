Conclusion (I): three metaphors and 
civilizing projects  

2
• “Minorities” have been subjected over the 
last few centuries to a series of attempts 
by dominant powers to transform them, to 
make them more like the transformers, or 
to “civilize” them.
• This is a kind of interaction between 
peoples, in which the civilizing center 
interacts with other groups (the peripheral 
peoples) in terms of a particular kind of 
inequality.

3
• Civilizers attempt to establish hegemony 
based on a particular formulation of the 
nature of ethnicity, many of whose 
characteristic recur from one project to the 
next.
• It notifies the peripheral peoples of their 
status in the system, and of why and how 
this project requires them to be civilized.

4
• The Confucians: beginning with an assumption 
of hierarchy based on the moral values.
• The Western missionary: technical-cultural, 
religions with the revealed truth of Christianity.
• The Communists: based on an avowedly 
scientific scale of material stages of social 
process, that tells each group exactly how far it 
needs to go to catch up with the civilizers.

The educational metaphor: ethnic 
minorities as children
• Seeing peripheral peoples as children, children 
represent both inferior and educable.
• The peripheral peoples represented as childlike are 
both inferior and civilizable, and the task of the 
center is to civilize them.
• The image of the peripheral is presented in official 
discourse in which the Han and minorities are 
described as xiongdi minzu 兄弟民族, or big-
brother/little-brother minzu 老大哥/小兄弟, with the 
Han.

The historical metaphor: 
ethnic minorities as ancient
• Peripheral peoples are ancient, 
unchanged, not so far along on the same 
scale as the people of the civilizer center.  
• But if the ancestor of the center were once 
as primitive as the periphery is now, then 
the project of trying to civilize them has 
some chance of success.

The Mosuo as the historical remainder

22
Almost any difference between the center 
and the peripheral peoples can be 
translated into a historical gap between 
the way things are done now and the way 
they were done at the evolutionary stage 
represented by the peripheral peoples.

23
• Confucian attitude: the center saw its relationship with 
the rest of the world as a process of making it more 
cultured, as a transformation from raw and untutored to 
fully civilized.
• Communist project: it has classified the population within 
China’s political borders into 56 minzu nationalities 民族. 
• The goal is to bring them to a universal standard of 
progress or modernity that exists independent of where 
the center might be on the historical scale at any given 
moment.
• Once this definitional stage was completed, it fell to the 
center to do the actual civilizing—the minzu gongzuo 民
族工作(work) of creating autonomous regions, 
implementing educational and developmental plans, 
bringing leaders of the peripheral peoples into the Party-
state apparatus that carries out the center’s project.  

24
To define ethnic consciousness as the awareness 
of belonging to an ethnic group:
1. Regard it as solidity, with the feature of sharing 
at least common descent and some kinds of 
common customs of habits that can serve as 
ethnic markers.
2. An ethnic group sees itself in opposition to other 
such groups, whose ancestors were different 
and whose customs and habits are foreign, 
strange.

25
• By portraying peripheral peoples as female, 
childlike, and historical, the Han (or European) 
center, which is by implicit or explicit contrast 
male, adult, and modern, legitimately assumes 
the task of civilizing, and with it the superior 
political and moral position from which the 
civilizing project can be carried out.
• The assumption of cultural superiority by the 
politically and economically powerful center and 
the use of that superiority, and the supposed 
benefits it can confer on the peripheral peoples, 
as an aspect of hegemony rule.
