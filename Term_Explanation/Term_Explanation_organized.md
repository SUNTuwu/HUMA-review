# 1. Culturalism and Manyi barbarians

**Logic:** Definition → Structure → Mechanism → Historical facts

**Definition:** (**culture-based community**) A **political** community based on **shared cultural values** and **Confucian theories**, rather than ethnicity.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.3)

**Structure:** (**Han self-identification**) Distinct from an exclusive ethnic-based community founded on a **self-description of a people as Han**.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.3)

**Mechanism:** (**education, imitation, and Sinification**) Through **education and imitation**, "barbarians" could become part of the cultural community and distinguish themselves from other "barbarians" who did not share these values.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.3)

> 与"生熟"不同，这个是**主观的文化上归属与否**；"生熟"是**客观的行政上管理范围的内外**。

**Historical facts:** (**four barbarian categories & Huaxia expansion**) The Huaxia group called the non-Huaxia peoples of four directions: **eastern Yi** (东夷), **western Rong** (西戎), **northern Di** (北狄), and **southern Man** (南蛮). The early "Chinese" state was small (the **Yellow River plain and the Yangtze River delta**). The rest of what is now China was ruled by groups called **barbarians** by the Huaxia — a process of **gradual expansion** (逐步扩展).
(Source: HUMA2635_2.pdf p.3)

# 2. Nation-state after 1911

**Logic:** Origin → Theory → Basis → Consequences

**Definition (Origin):** (**Western-style nationalism**) After the Qing, republican leaders were **heavily influenced by Western-style nationalism** and used it to found a new state.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.11)

**Structure (KMT Theory):** (**unique community / empire vs nation / tradition vs modernity**) The KMT viewed the nation as a **unique and unprecedented** form of community, which finds its place in the oppositions between **empire and nation**, **tradition and modernity**.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.13)

**Mechanism (Basis):** (**modernization theory / collective subject of modernity**) Western theories of nationalism are deeply embedded in **modernization theory**. This theory regards the nation as a **collective subject of modernity**, but it **obscures** the nature of national identity.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf pp.11, 13)

> 现代性理论**掩盖**了民族身份认同的本质。

**Historical facts (Consequences):**
- (**novel consciousness**) National identity is a **radically novel** form of consciousness, and nationalism is a form of identification based on the **modern nation-state system**.
  (Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.13)
- (**only legitimate polity**) The nation-state system regards the **nation-state as the only legitimate** form of polity.
  (Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.14)
- (**penetration of state power**) The ideology of the nation-state system has sanctioned the **penetration of state power** into areas once dominated by local structures.
  (Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.14)
- (**popular sovereignty / source of legitimacy**) The statement of **the people as sovereign** remains the single most important **source of legitimacy** of the nation-state within China.
  (Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.8)

# 3. Debates between Constitutional monarchists and Revolutionaries (pre-1911)

**Logic:** Core debate → Constitutional monarchist view → Revolutionary view → Revolutionary theoretical tool

**Definition (Core debate):** (**culture vs. race**) The foundation of China's future political community: **culture vs. race**.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.6)

**Structure (Constitutional monarchists' view):** (**shared culture**) (Led by **Kang Youwei**) They inherited the **Confucian culturalist notion** of community, composed of people with a **shared culture** and **not restricted to a race or ethnic group**.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.6)

**Mechanism (Revolutionaries' view):** (**Social Darwinism**) (Led by **Zhang Binglin**, **Zou Rong**, and **Wang Jingwei**) They adopted a **Social Darwinist** conception of the **survival of the fittest races**.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.6)

**Historical facts (Revolutionary theoretical tool):** (**Han ethnocentrism**) Used the **"survival of the fittest"** idea to justify the old **ethnocentric celebration of the Han**. This marked a **sharp ideological split** between an **inclusive, culture-based vision** of China and an **exclusive, race-based one**.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.6)

# 4. Chiang Kai-shek's arguments about peoples in China

**Logic:** Core claim → Explanation of differences → Political conclusion

**Definition (Core claim):** (**same racial stock**) "Our various clans (氏族) actually belong to the **same racial stock** (种族)."
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.17)

**Structure (Explanation of differences):** (**religion and geography**) The designation of **five peoples** (汉满蒙回藏) in China is **not due to differences of race or blood** but to **religion and geographical environment**.
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.17)

**Mechanism:** (implicit — **minimizing ethnic distinctions**) By attributing ethnic differences to **religion and geography**, Chiang **reinforced a narrative of inherent racial unity** among all groups, including **Mongols, Manchus, Uyghurs and Tibetans**, under a single Chinese nationality.
(Inferred from HUMA2635_5.pdf p.17)

**Historical facts (Political conclusion):** (**single cultural-political whole**) The Chinese people form a **"Single Cultural and Political whole"** (统一的民族与政治整体).
(Source: HUMA2635_5.pdf p.17)

# 5. The Dalai Lama and the Panchen Lama

**Logic:** Recognition & Reincarnation → Spiritual identity → Structural tension → Institutional regulation → Historical conflict

**Definition:** (**temporal and spiritual rule /政教合一 / reincarnation system**) In Tibet, the **temporal as well as the spiritual rule** of the Dalai Lama was recognized. The system of **reincarnation** was set up in **1447 during the Ming dynasty**.
(Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.6)

**Structure (Spiritual identity):** (**Avalokitesvara 观世音菩萨 / Amitabha 阿弥陀佛**) The Dalai Lama is the reincarnation of **Avalokitesvara**. The **Fifth Dalai Lama** proclaimed the Panchen Lama the reincarnation of **Amitabha**, who was the **teacher of Avalokitesvara**.
(Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.9)

**Mechanism (Structural tension):** (**spiritual vs. temporal — inverted hierarchy**) Although **Amitabha is higher** in the Buddhist view — and some have argued the Panchen Lama is therefore **spiritually superior** — the Panchen Lama's **temporal powers were minimal**, while those of the Dalai Lama were recognized as **supreme**.
(Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.9)

**Historical facts:**
- (**Qing reforms / commoner reincarnation / Amban**) Since powerful families often sought to have the Dalai Lama declared reincarnated in their own families, choosing the reincarnation became **power politics**. Reform by the Qing government required the Dalai Lama be reincarnated in a **commoner's family**. **The resident Amban** (驻藏大臣) was responsible for supervising these regulations. The net effect began to **bureaucratize** Tibetan administration and to **increase the power** of the Dalai Lama relative to noble families.
  (Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.7)
- (**13th Dalai Lama / 9th Panchen Lama / Britain-China alliances**) The **13th Dalai Lama** used an alliance with **Britain** to strengthen his control over semi-feudal components, including the areas of the **9th Panchen Lama**. This led the Panchen Lama to turn to the **Chinese central government** for aid against their common enemies — the Dalai Lama and Britain.
  (Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.9)

# 6. The formulation of historical materialism

**Logic:** Base → Mechanism → Stages → Stage markers → Example

**Definition:** (**superstructure based on economic base**) A **superstructure** is based on an **economic base**.
(Source: HUMA2635_6.pdf p.7)

**Structure:** (**modes of production determine social forms**) The forms of the family (as cultural system / superstructure) changed as a result of changes in the **modes of production**.
(Source: HUMA2635_6.pdf p.7)

**Mechanism (Five historical stages):** Human history proceeds through distinct stages: **Primitive → Slavery → Feudal → Capitalist → Socialist → Communist**. Each stage consists of a complex of related cultural traits; people displaying certain traits must be representative of the **particular stage** in which those traits occur.
(Source: HUMA2635_6.pdf p.8)

> 写作five读作六（共产是结果/目标，不算stage）

**Historical facts (Example):** The **Mosuo's matriliny** (摩梭母系制) means that they are at the **second substage of the primitive stage**.
(Source: HUMA2635_6.pdf p.8)

# 7. Identification of nationalities (民族识别)

**Logic:** Theory (Stalin) → Scope of classification → Outcome examples

**Definition (Theory):** (**language, territory, economy, and psychology**) Nationality is a **historically constituted, stable community of people**, formed on the basis of a **common language, territory, economic life, and psychological make-up** manifested in a common culture.
(Source: HUMA2635_6.pdf p.25)

**Structure (Scope of classification):** (**400+ → 55**) By **1979**, the number of **55 nationalities** was reached, identified from more than **400 ethnic groups**.
(Source: HUMA2635_6.pdf p.25)

**Mechanism:** (**objective criteria + state categorization**) The identification involved both **objective criteria** (Stalin's definition) and **state categorization** — not purely based on cultural distinctiveness.
(Inferred from HUMA2635_6.pdf p.25; HUMA2635_1.pdf p.20)

**Historical facts (Examples):**
- (**Hakkas = Han**) The **Hakkas** (客家), though culturally, linguistically, and socially distinct, are classified as part of the **Han majority**.
  (Source: HUMA2635_1.pdf p.20)
- (**Mosuo = Naxi branch**) The **Mosuo** (摩梭) are not recognized as a separate nationality; they are considered a **branch of the Naxi** (纳西族).
  (Source: HUMA2635_1.pdf p.20)

# 8. The Sufism orders in China

**Logic:** Arrival → Institutional form → Internal transmission → Spatial practice → Cultural expression → Example

**Definition (Historical arrival):** (**late 17th century / early Qing**) Sufism did not begin to make a **substantial impact** in China until the **late 17th century**. Many Sufi reforms spread throughout **Northwest China** during the early decades of the Qing dynasty.
(Source: HUMA2635_7.pdf p.38)

**Structure (Institutional form):** (**menhuan 门宦 / saintly lineages**) Sufi orders were gradually **institutionalized** into such forms as **menhuan**, meaning **"saintly lineages"** — combining blood-based secular power with sectarian religious authority.
(Source: HUMA2635_7.pdf pp.38, 43)

**Mechanism (Internal transmission):** (**baraka 口唤/神圣祝福 / biological descent vs. teacher-disciple**) Divine Blessing (**baraka**) flows from God to the charismatic leader (**shaykh**). Baraka transmission could occur by **biological descent** — thus sanctioning menhuan — or by **teacher-disciple transmission**. Questioning the legitimacy of transmission can lead to **conflict, violence, and schism**.
(Source: HUMA2635_7.pdf pp.42-44)

> Four major orders: **Qadiriyya** (嘎得林耶), **Jahriyya** (哲赫忍耶), **Khufiyya** (虎夫耶), and **Kubrawiyya** (庫布忍耶).

**Historical facts:**
- (**gongbei 拱北 / spatial practice**) Each saint's tomb had a shrine, or **gongbei** (拱北), and the main shrines became **centers of devotional activity**.
  (Source: HUMA2635_7.pdf p.44)
- (**syncretism** / cultural expression) Chinese Sufi mysticism is similar to **Daoism**. **Confucian moral tenets, Daoist mystical concepts, and Buddhist folk rituals** infused with new Islamic content pervade Qadiriyya Sufism.
  (Source: HUMA2635_7.pdf p.48)
- (**Jahriyya / Ma Mingxin**) The **Jahriyya** order was founded by **Ma Mingxin** (马明心, 1719-1781), who studied in the Middle East and Yemen for **16 years**. After disputes escalated and Qing troops arrested Ma Mingxin in **1781**, uprisings erupted. Following their failure, the Jahriyya became more secretive and dispersed, leading to **five main branch orders**.
  (Source: HUMA2635_7.pdf pp.48-49)

# 9. The Oases Structure in Xinjiang

**Logic:** Defining feature → Major oases & characteristics → Social contrasts → Overall significance

**Definition (Feature):** (**diversity through isolation**) Social and cultural diversity are fostered throughout history by the **great distances separating the oases** from one another.
(Source: HUMA2635_8.pdf p.37)

**Structure (Major oases & towns):** The most significant oases are **Kashgar** (喀什), **Khotan** (和阗), **Turpan** (吐鲁番), **Hami** (哈密), **Ili** (伊犁), and **Urumchi** (乌鲁木齐 — the capital).
(Source: HUMA2635_8.pdf p.37)

**Mechanism (Each oasis's role):**
- **Kashgar** — (**Islam heartland & conservative**) Islam arrived by **950 C.E.** It remains the **Islamic heartland** of Xinjiang; more **conservative** Islamic life, women completely covered in brown cloth.
  (Source: HUMA2635_8.pdf pp.39, 52)
- **Khotan** — (**Silk Road southern branch**) A major cultural center in Central Asia, key position on the **southern branch of the Silk Road**, linking to **South Asia and Tibet** via **Karakorum Mountains**.
  (Source: HUMA2635_8.pdf pp.43-44)
- **Turpan** — (**Uyghur cultural center**) The **historic center of Uyghur culture**; settled by the Uyghur non-Islamic **Gaochang kingdom** after **840 A.D.**; women only wear headscarves, viewed as more trustworthy by Han.
  (Source: HUMA2635_8.pdf pp.50, 52)
- **Ili** — (**richest & Kazakhstan-oriented**) Always considered the **richest area** in Xinjiang; geographically isolated from southern Xinjiang and physically oriented toward **Kazakhstan**; posed the greatest **Turkic political threat** to Chinese government.
  (Source: HUMA2635_8.pdf pp.45, 47)

**Historical facts (Overall significance):** Oases identities and **oases economic-political structure** set the **basic feature** for Xinjiang society.
(Source: HUMA2635_8.pdf p.53)

# 10. The Yi and the Miao

**Logic:** Internal diversity → Yi (language + structure) → Miao (label + subgroups + identity) → Competition pattern

**Definition (Yi diversity — language):** (**less than 50% shared vocabulary**) Languages are **not mutually understandable**; there is only **20-42% shared vocabulary** between the Northern Dialect standard and other five regional dialects.
(Source: HUMA2635_9.pdf p.5)

**Structure (Yi structural contrast):** (**Yunnan vs. Liangshan castes**) **Yunnan Yi** feature lowland agriculture, Han-style housing, and **patrilocal** (从父居住) marriage; **Nuosu (Liangshan Yi)** feature **neolocal** (从新居住) marriage, a highly developed **patriclan** (父系氏族) system, strict **social castes** (种姓/等级), and are **stratum-endogamous** (阶层内通婚).
(Source: HUMA2635_9.pdf p.9)

**Mechanism (Miao historical label):** (**interchangeable barbarian label**) In the Ming period, the term "Miao" was applied to many indigenous peoples, used **interchangeably with "Man" or "Yi"** , all meaning **"barbarians"** .
(Source: HUMA2635_9.pdf p.16)

**Historical facts:**
- (**color-based subgroups**) Miao subgroups distinguished by dominant color of women's costumes: **White Miao, Black Miao, Green Miao, Red Miao, and Hua Miao**.
  (Source: HUMA2635_9.pdf pp.17, 19)
- (**externally named identity / no internal contact**) Because of a lack of contact among the scattered subgroups, these peoples are called Miao only because they have **always been called so by others**. They **do not possess a common self-identity**.
  (Source: HUMA2635_9.pdf p.22)
- (**external competition pattern**) Competition for political office or economic advantages is usually **not between subgroups of Miao**, but between **local Miao and Han or other ethnic groups** in the area.
  (Source: HUMA2635_9.pdf p.22)

# 11. The Tea and Horse Trade (茶馬貿易)

**Logic:** Definition → Tang (informal) → Song (institutionalized) → Ming (expanded + regulated) → Tibetan significance

**Definition:** (**tea-for-horses barter**) **Barter** (物物交换) commercial trade between **agriculturists of interior provinces** and **nomads of Tibet and Mongolia**; mainly **tea and horses** but not limited to these.
(Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.19)

**Structure (Tang — informal):** (**no formal institution**) Only **sporadic** (零星) and **irregular** trade, usually using silk; **no trade institution** formed.
(Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.19)

**Mechanism (Song — institutionalized):** (**茶馬司 / Xining period**) During **Xining period** (熙寧, 1068-1077), government set up a **tea farm department** (Chengdu) and a **horse buying office** (Lintao, Gansu); the official trade unit became known as the **"division of tea and horse trade"** (茶馬司).
(Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.19)

**Historical facts:**
- (**Ming — expanded + regulated**) Established **12 tea and horse trade divisions** in northwest and southwest regions. Also implemented a **tea patrol system** (巡查制度), regularly dispatching officials to **Shaanxi and Sichuan** to supervise trade and **prohibit private tea** (prevent smuggling).
  (Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.19)
- (**Tibetan staple since 7th century**) Tea has been a **staple** (主食) in Tibetan homes and culture since the **7th century A.D.** ; compressed into **rock-hard bricks** kept for decades; **buttered tea** believed to warm the body, fight hunger, and aid digestion.
  (Source: HUMA2635_10.pdf p.16)

# 12. Ethnicity classification in Hong Kong

**Logic:** Classification & contrast with mainland → Population → Ethnic hierarchy → Semi-ethnocracy → Examples

**Definition (Classification):** (**self-identification, UN standards vs. minzu system**) Determined by **self-identification** based on cultural origins, nationality, color, and language, following **UN recommendations**; it does **not** use the state-recognized official (**minzu**) nationalities system of mainland China.
(Source: HUMA2635_12.pdf pp.12, 22)

**Structure (Population):** (**Cantonese dominant, major minorities**) **Cantonese speakers** are the dominant majority; major minority groups include **Filipinos**, **Indonesians**, **South Asians**, **Mixed**, and **Whites**.
(Source: HUMA2635_12.pdf pp.3, 7)

**Mechanism (Ethnic hierarchy):** (**internal Chinese hierarchy**) A hierarchy exists where **Shanghainese** are **elites**, **Chaozhou/Minnanese** are **intermediate**, **Hakka** are **low-income**, **South Asians** occupy **lower class**, and **Southeast Asian domestic workers** form the **base**.
(Source: HUMA2635_12.pdf p.3)

**Historical facts (Semi-ethnocracy + Examples):**
- (**Semi-ethnocracy**) One ethnic group **dominates without total exclusion**; non-dominant elites occupy scattered positions of influence; **exclusionary laws and norms (hegemony)** disable other groups.
  (Source: HUMA2635_12.pdf pp.10-11)
- (**two-year contract & two-week rule**) Foreign domestic workers are restricted to **two-year contracts** and cannot change employment; if terminated, they must **leave within two weeks** unless proving non-responsibility.
  (Source: HUMA2635_12.pdf pp.14, 16)
- (**SAR passport exclusion / three-year rule**) Non-ethnic Chinese permanent residents **ineligible for SAR passports** even if born in Hong Kong; non-Chinese **lose right of abode** by living abroad for **over three years**, while PRC citizens never lose it.
  (Source: HUMA2635_12.pdf p.18)
